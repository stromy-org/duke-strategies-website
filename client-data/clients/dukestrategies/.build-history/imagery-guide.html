Visual Philosophy

Why Bridges?

Duke Strategies builds bridges between business leaders and their stakeholders. The bridge is not a metaphor we chose — it is the structural reality of what strategic communications does. Every campaign, every stakeholder engagement, every crisis navigated is a span built between where an organisation is and where it needs to be.

We photograph bridges in black and white to convey authority, permanence, and timelessness. These are not decorative structures — they are engineered solutions to real problems. The monochrome treatment strips away distraction, revealing form, tension, and strength.

The coral overlay (#FF7F66 at 55% opacity, multiply blend) introduces Duke Strategies into the image. It is warmth laid over steel — approachability without sacrificing seriousness. The result is photography that feels both institutional and distinctive.

Imagery Principles

Six Rules for Brand Photography

Every image in the Duke Strategies visual system follows these principles. They ensure consistency across presentations, documents, social media, and digital properties.

01
Black & White Only
No colour photography enters the brand system. All source images are desaturated to pure grayscale before any brand treatment. This creates a unified, authoritative base.
02
Architectural Subjects
Bridges, infrastructure, and geometric structures. We photograph engineered spans — cable-stayed, suspension, arch, pedestrian. The subject reinforces the brand metaphor.
03
High Contrast & Moody
Deep blacks, bright highlights, atmospheric conditions. Night photography, fog, dramatic lighting. The mood conveys gravitas and sophistication.
04
Global Bridges, Dutch Roots
The library is anchored by Dutch and European bridges reflecting the firm's heritage, expanded with iconic global structures. Rotterdam's Erasmus Bridge leads; Brooklyn Bridge, Golden Gate silhouettes, and modern cable-stayed spans round out the 20-image collection.
05
55% Coral Overlay
Brand-treated images receive a #FF7F66 coral tint at 55% opacity using multiply blend mode. This transforms generic B&W into distinctively Duke Strategies imagery.
06
White Logo on Overlaid Images
When placing the Duke Strategies logo on brand-treated imagery, always use the white variant. The coral overlay provides sufficient contrast for white elements.

Visual Vocabulary

Subject Guidelines

A clear boundary between what enters the Duke Strategies visual system and what stays outside it.

✓
Use
  • Bridges and overpasses
  • Infrastructure and engineering
  • Geometric architecture
  • Aerial and drone views
  • Night photography
  • Stark contrast and deep shadows
  • Fog, mist, atmospheric conditions
  • Steel, concrete, industrial materials
  • Dutch and European landmarks
×
Avoid
  • Colour photography
  • People and portraits
  • Nature and landscapes
  • Stock office scenes
  • Illustrations and clip art
  • Abstract art
  • Busy or complex patterns
  • Warm, cozy, or domestic scenes
  • Low-contrast or flat images
