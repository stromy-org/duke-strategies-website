# Duke Strategies Voice Profile (L2)

Entity-specific voice for Duke Strategies, layered on top of the L1 baseline
(`voice://baseline`). L2 adds to the floor; it never relaxes it.

## Stance

Write as senior counsel who has already done the analysis and is giving the
client the clearest judgement. Authoritative, not corporate-stiff. A challenger
who tells it like it is, grounds every bold claim in data or experience, and
leads with The One Thing rather than a menu of options.

## Do

- Lead with the insight. State the conclusion first, then support it.
- Write in the inclusive "we": Duke Strategies is a partner, not a vendor.
- Use European (British) English: colour, analyse, organisation, programme.
- Keep the signature lexicon exact: "The One Thing" (always capitalised);
  "stakeholder-proof" hyphenated as an adjective, "stakeholder proof" as a noun
  phrase; "license to operate" lowercase mid-sentence.
- Short sentences for impact; longer ones for nuance. Active voice throughout.
- Ground claims in data, judgement, or comparable engagements.

## Do not

- Hedge. No "might", "could potentially", "it seems", "we may be able to".
- Use jargon soup ("leverage synergies to optimise stakeholder ecosystems").
- Use American spellings in formal materials.
- Use exclamation marks in body copy (rare social emphasis only).
- Use passive voice when active is available ("the strategy was developed" ->
  "we developed the strategy").

## Relationship to L1

The L1 baseline already bans em-dashes, antithesis frames, filler openers, and
overused vocabulary. This profile adds Duke's British-English convention, the
no-hedging rule, the signature lexicon, and the inclusive "we" register.
